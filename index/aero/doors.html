5.Loads |

Non-Plug Door | On this door, load travels from the hinges out through the latches. The primary load path is the latch frames for the hoop load, and the outer skin for the shear load.

Outer Skin | is a major load transferring part and critical element. This skin repair is located at the bottom corner of the door. At the door lower edge the hoop tension loads are transferred from the skin to the frames and then to the latch fittings. Therefore, at the repair location the skin stress is lower than the max skin hoop stress below the hinges.
Conservatively, the highest reference stress of 9.58 ksi is used from the SSID F-37 back up analysis (see Ref.i) | Critical location according to SSID F-37 is between STA 1787 and STA 1808 and S-9 and S-11 – zone close to hinges | This location is remote to the zone considered in this analysis, therefore, the stress rating of S = 9.58 ksi is conservative for fatigue analysis of the damaged location (refer for Table 1 and Figure 6) |

Inner Skin | The inner skin is only on the top portion of the door. The primary purpose of the inner skin is to provide a torsional stability [it creates a torque box] for the door during the door operation (open/close). The inner skin carries pressure and flight loads due to its attachment to the door structure.

The lower beam and shear fittings transfer the shear load from the outer skin out of the door through the latches, and the latch frames are tied directly into the latch support fittings, and latches to transfer the hoop load out of the door.

The intercostals primarily transfer the longitudinal door shear load from the outer skin out of the door.

Plug Door | The passenger entry door is a plug type door; therefore, the primary design load case for the repair area is beam bending due to differential pressure.

Outer Skin | The primary load in the outer skin of plug-type doors is beam bending due to internal pressure. | This bending load is zero at the edges of the door; therefore, the stress at the deviation location is small. |

Inner Skin | This deviation is located entirely on the compressive side of the beam neutral axis; therefore the structure is loaded primarily in compression. | The deviations are acceptable for fatigue and damage tolerance due to the low likelihood of crack initiation and/or growth.

Beam OC | Beam #3 ultimate stress at OS = 22.78 ksi. (Ref.i). | The fatigue pressure is 8.6 psi + 0.24 negative aerodynamic pressure = 8.84 psi (Ref.1). | Ultimate pressure = 18.2 psi. Ratio = 8.84/18.2= 0.486. | Fatigue stress = 22.78*0.486 = 11.07 ksi.

Hinge cutout | The main load path for the damage location is on up-down direction because the area is between two skin cutouts one for the hinge and the other for the handle pan. The fatigue stress for the undamaged production structure was calculated in Ref.1 and equals f_fat=8.62 ksi.
